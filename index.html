Whats kelp?

Kelp is one of 10,000 species of macroalgae. It's native to the cold waters of the Northeastern seaboard, and is full of vitamins and trace minerals like Vitamin A, Vitamin E, Thiamine (B1), Riboflavin (B2), Iron, Iodine, Calcium, Potassium, Magnesium, Omegas, and Zinc.

The world's first carbon negative snack

Our ocean-farmed kelp is a zero-input crop that grows without dry land, fresh water, fertilizer or feed. Kelp also has the ability to sequester carbon and nitrogen from our seas 5-20x more effectively than land-based plants do from the air.

In 2019, our Kelp Jerky product alone uses 40,000 pounds of regeneratively ocean farmed kelp responsible for pulling 2,000 pounds (1 ton) of carbon from our oceans.

Comparatively, eating 300 beef burgers creates the same amount of carbon! In fact, animal agriculture, uses of our planet's fresh water, of our dry land, and is the 2nd largest contributor to greenhouse gas emissions in the world.
